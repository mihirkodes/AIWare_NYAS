🔵 Intermediate Capstone · Project 2

The "Glyph-Guardian"

Use AI to create a mnemonic story that helps early readers remember how to correctly orient easily-confused letters like b, d, p, and q — combining narrative, rhyme, and visual design.

Part 1 — Project Planning Worksheet (The Story)

Template: "Act as a children's author. For the letter [__], create a 2-line rhyme about a [theme] where the shape of the animal/character matches the shape of the letter."

Part 2 — User Feedback Form (The "Stickiness" Test)

Part 3 — Grading Rubric

Criteria 5 — Excellent 3–4 — Good 1–2 — Needs Work
Mnemonic Effectiveness The story creates a perfect "anchor" for the letter's shape; impossible to flip. The story is good, but the "shape" connection is a little weak. The story doesn't actually explain the shape of the letter.
Visual Design The drawing and letter are integrated perfectly into a "single image." The drawing is next to the letter, but they aren't fully "one thing." The drawing is messy or doesn't match the story.
Student Focus The language is 100% appropriate for a 1st–3rd grader. Most words are easy, but 1–2 are too complex for a young child. The story is written for an adult, not a child.
Testing Insight The team changed the story/rhyme after a kid said it was confusing. The team asked for feedback but didn't make changes. No evidence of testing with a real learner.
